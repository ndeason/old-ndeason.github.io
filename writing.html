Free Resource: Parasitic Diseases, 6th Edition
12/1/16

Parasites Without Borders is an organization founded by three infectious disease scientists with the goal of helping to better educate “all those in a position to apply medical knowledge directly to populations most in need of solutions to alleviate the burden of parasitic diseases.” In keeping with that aim, the team has produced an extensive textbook compiling the biological and clinical knowledge of human parasitic diseases. The new 6th edition is up-to-date and incorporates hundreds of citations from the most recent literature. Best of all, they are offering a free PDF download of the book to everyone, everywhere.

    Infectious Diseases in America Before European Contact
    8/2/16

    Christopher Columbus reached the Americas on October 12, 1492, in what is now the Bahamas. On board his ships were more than 80 men, stores of food and weapons, and of course, millions of infectious microbes. Diseases brought to the New World during the Columbian Exchange included smallpox, measles, and mumps, among many others. We know that these infections were devastating to the non-immune indigenous populations, killing as many as 95% of all Native Americans in the 150 years after Columbus landed. However, much less is said about the diseases that were already present in the Americas before European arrival, and how one disfiguring sexually transmitted disease in particular raced across Europe after supposedly being transplanted from the Americas.
