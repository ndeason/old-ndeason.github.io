Free Resource: Parasitic Diseases, 6th Edition

Parasites Without Borders is an organization founded by three infectious disease scientists with the goal of helping to better educate “all those in a position to apply medical knowledge directly to populations most in need of solutions to alleviate the burden of parasitic diseases.”
 
In keeping with that aim, the team has produced an extensive textbook compiling biological and clinical knowledge of human parasitic diseases. The new 6th edition is up-to-date and incorporates hundreds of citations from the most recent literature. Best of all, they are offering a free PDF download of the book to everyone, everywhere. They hope to get this book into the hands of every medical student in the country.
 
Click here to get your copy of Parasitic Diseases.

If you’re like me, you can use the textbook to help solve the “case of the week” on the This Week in Parasitism podcast, in which Dr. Daniel Griffin (an author of the textbook) presents a real life case study of an infection and gives clues that help the listeners identify the causative parasite.
 
Happy reading!
