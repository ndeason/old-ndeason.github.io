Our Take on #VanLife

August 8, 2017


For the next month, our van is our life. It contains everything we own, including our bodies, and we have a pretty sweet setup if you ask us.

The most crucial modification we made was to remove the back seats of the van. We reserved two sides of the resulting space for large containers of hiking and camping gear, and covered the rest of the floor space with a single layer of large USPS boxes full of our clothes. On top of that flat layer we laid mats and blankets to make napping spot with plenty of room to fully recline. Here’s Jackie relaxing after we parked at an overlook on the Blue Ridge Parkway.

Initially, we expected to use a car-top cargo carrier, but we ended up having enough room inside for all our things. Plus we didn’t want to worry about the carrier opening up on the road and leaving our underwear strewn all over Interstate 40.

The coolest addition to our van is probably a $30 12V stove. Yep, this thing plugs right into the cigarette lighter and can cook everything from soups to eggs. So far we’ve only used it for making rice, but we’re hoping to get adventurous and try chili soon. This device has already paid for itself by allowing us to make super cheap meals on the road without stopping.

Other features include a line for drying laundry and a three prong power inverter for charging laptops, cameras, etc.

Do you have any road trip vehicle tips? Let us know on Instagram! Jackie | Nick
